World Bank Health Data Analysis

In this project, I wanted to visualize some of the healthcare data available on World Bank open data. Some of the factors I looked at include maternal mortality, infant mortality, life expectancy, death rate (mortality rate), government expenditure in healthcare, and adult obesity. I used world bank api (wbdata) to acquire the desired data following the indicators dictionary linked here

After acquiring the data, I used pandas to read it in form of a dataframe and perform simple data cleaning. Finally, I use Matplotlip, Seaborn and Plotly libraries to create some visualizations.

The plot above shows world maternal mortality by region in 2016. The graph shows that countries in Sub-saharan Africa have the highest levels of maternal mortality while countries in North America, Europe and Central Asia have the lowest maternal mortality.

This plot shows ten countries with the highest maternal mortality in 2016. From the diagram, we can tell that aside from Afghanistan, all the other nine countries with high maternal mortality, are all Sub-sahara African countries.

This plot shows the ten countries with the lowest maternal mortality rates in 2016. It is worth noting that no North American country makes it to the top ten.

I then use plotly to create interactive graphs. The plot below shows world life expectancy by country. Sub-saharan African countries have the lowest life expectancy.
